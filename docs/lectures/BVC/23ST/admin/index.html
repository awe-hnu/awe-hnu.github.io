General remarks

In this course you will develop a technological artefact that creates value for a specific organisation (e.g. prototyping a new product, improving a process, supporting a decision).

You will use the theoretical knowledge and skills you have acquired and developed in the first semester.

You will also be introduced to current methodological and theoretical knowledge. This will help you to carry out the real-world problem and to reflect on the process and the results.

It is very important that you prepare yourself for the classes (see Table 1 for details).

Contents

This module will allow you to apply your learning from your undergraduate degree as well as from the first semester of your masters programme.

To support understanding and transfer of topics, additional knowledge of core concepts and related methods, e.g.:

  • Identifying (customer) needs (e.g. Design Thinking)
  • The generation and development of ideas
  • Business value sources and characteristics
  • Essential aspects of (digital) innovation
  • Challenges in implementing digital innovations (changing, adopting & using)
  • Capture and measurement of value from IT/digital innovations

will be taught as well.

Learning objectives

During this course, you should advance your skills in the following areas:

  • Understand the mechanisms by which IT creates business value
  • How to conceptualise, design and implement IT solutions that create value in a specific context.
  • How to apply and contextualise existing knowledge and understanding of disruptive technologies in the context of a specific organisational environment
  • Problem-solving skills to analyze the particularities and complexities of different project contexts
  • Reflecting on social and ethical responsibilities and making responsible decisions with incomplete or limited information

Effort

You will gain 5 ECTS for this course. This equals approx. 150 hours workload, of which you need most to pass the course.

  • Live sessions (approx. 20 hours)
  • Preparation of the live sessions (approx. 20 hours)
  • Project work and documentation (remaining time)

Please prepare your schedule accordingly.

Grading

During the semester you will be required to work within a group on a business challenge posed by a partner of EKIPA.

You present your approach to the challenge and to create value by means of IT in a presentation.

You detail your approach and solution in a seminar paper.

Details regarding the assignment can be found here.

The grade is the weighted average of the group score (60%) and the individual contributions (40%); the individual contributions must be indicated.
