Introduction

General remarks

You will gain an overview of the interplay of sustainability and IS, learn about the complexity and trade offs involved, and discover how IS impacts the dimensions of sustainability through specific examples.

Learning objectives

During this course, you should advance your skills in the following areas:

  • understanding of the concept of sustainable development (SD) and its significance in contemporary society,
  • knowledge of key principles and frameworks related to sustainable development,
  • Awareness and appraisal of different sustainability metrics and measures.
  • appreciation of the role of information systems (IS) in addressing sustainability challenges,
  • understanding of relevant concepts and theories,
  • critical thinking skills to identify complex challenges and possible solutions,
  • communication of conclusions and the underlying knowledge,
  • and social and ethical responsibilities in uncertain and ambiguous contexts.

Effort

You will gain 5 ECTS for this course. This equals approx. 90 hours workload, of which you need most to pass the course.

  • Lectures (approx. 20 hours)
  • Preparation (approx. 10 hours)
  • Presentation (remaining time)

It is very important that you reserve enough time to attend the classes AND prepare your presentation (see Table 1 for details).

Examination

Graded will be your description of an example for IS for SD, a theory-guided analysis of the process and outcomes as well as your ability do critically discuss the example.

The grade will be a combination of

  • your presentation of your case
  • the discussion after the presentation
  • and your contributions to the discussion of your fellow students‚Äô presentations

Details regarding the assignment can be found here.

Schedule

It is of importance that you prepare yourself for the sessions and work on the presentation continually. Please prepare your schedule accordingly.
