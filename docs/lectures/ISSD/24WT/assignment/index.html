Task

During the semester you will be required to analyse an example on how IS impacts sustainable development.

To avoid duplicates and ensure alignment with my expectations, you should discuss the topic in advance with me.

You need to do research on

  • the characteristics of the information system,
  • the dimension of sustainable development it is related to,
  • the process and outcomes,
  • complexities and trade offs,
  • as well as relevant concepts and theories to frame your findings.

You can include grey literature1, but you need to consider at least five (5) scientific papers.

Deliverables

You need to hold a presentation of 10 minutes and to discuss your insights in a Q&A.

  • Upload your presentation slides (.pdf) and a declaration on the use of GenAI (.pdf, use this template) via Moodle until the deadline
  • Use following naming scheme:
    • ISSD_WT24_Surname-Name_presentation.pdf
    • ISSD_WT24_Surname-Name_declaration.pdf
  • You will present the slides uploaded to Moodle (no late updates)
  • For the deadline and the presentation dates, please see the schedule

Grading

The presentation of your findings (9-10 min., 70%)
+ the discussion (~ 4 min., 20%)
+ your contributions to other presentations (10%).

The grade will be a combination of

  • your presentation of your findings
    (9-10 minutes, 70% of your grade),
  • the discussion after the presentation
    (approx. 4 minutes, 20% of your grade),
  • and your contributions to the discussion of your fellow students‚Äô presentations
    (10% of your grade).

Evaluation criteria

An very good presentation has the following characteristics.

  • Clearly defines the approach to use IS for sustainable development
  • Accurately describes the characteristics of the IS, the dimension of sustainable development, the process and outcomes as well as complexities and trade offs
  • Relates the findings to relevant concepts and theories
  • Demonstrates critical thinking in considering the role of IS for sustainable development
  • Is well-organized and easy to follow
  • Presents information clearly and concisely
  • Speaks confidently and professionally
  • Integrates research from at least five (5) scientific papers
  • Accurately cites and references all sources
  • Demonstrates a clear and depth understanding of the contents

10% of your grade depends on how you engage in the discussion of other presentations.

  • Points are given for each thoughtful question or comment2 you make during the Q&A of other presentations
  • Additional points are given for the best question per presentation

Scientific literature

In your presentation, you need to consider at least five scientific papers.

  • Select only papers that are peer-reviewed.
  • Recommendation is to focus the search on high-quality IS journals3, such as
    • Senior Scholars‚Äô Basket of Journals
    • A-ranked or B-ranked journals in the VHB-JOURQUAL3 ranking
  • Use Web of Science, Google Scholar or the journals‚Äô archives to search for literature
  • Use the citation count as primary quality indicator (rule of thumb: the more cited, the more significant is the paper).
  • Look for literature reviews that summarize the current state of knowledge.
