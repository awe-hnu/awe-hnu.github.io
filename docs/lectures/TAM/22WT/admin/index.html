Motivation

Technology has changed significantly over the past 50 years. However, for too many CIOs, the way it is managed has not! Brian Adams, CIO Worley Parsons

Expectations

What do you expect to learn in this course?

Contents

IT management today is challenged by fast technological change, new digital opportunities, growing cost pressure, and increasing need to comply with business and regulatory requirements.

. . .

State-of-the-art IT management needs to ensure that organizations are capable of managing technologies and applications more agile, flexible and efficient than ever before.

. . .

Being able to achieve this requires not only a good understanding of the opportunities the digital age and new technologies offer, but also a good understanding of the core business of the company and sound IT cost management capabilities.

This course offers you the possibility to gain a solid know-how regarding theories, methods and tools for

  • selecting the right technologies and applications (e.g., business-IT alignment, technology foresight and evaluation, program management)
  • creating and managing efficient sociotechnical solutions (e.g., enterprise architecture management)
  • delivering technologies and applications efficiently (e.g., scaled agility, demand and service management, cybersecurity risk management)
  • ensuring IT-business value realization (e.g., cost accounting, data governance management)

Learning outcomes

Upon successful completion of this course, you will have advanced knowledge and skills in following areas:

  • Capacity to explain why technology and application management (TAM) is key for organizations to survive in the digital age
  • Understanding of the key challenges of TAM
  • Expertise regarding the main frameworks for managing IT in organizations
  • Capacity to design end-to-end TAM approaches by selecting and adapting available approaches, methods and tools
  • Ability to make responsible decisions with incomplete or limited information

Learning set-up

This course will be taught using an self-directed learning approach. It combines presence teaching, self-learning components, and application of newly acquired knowledge and skills.

. . .

The focus of the presence-teaching sessions is on giving you input to selected topics, answering your questions, recommending reads, and giving you feedback on your learning progress.

. . .

Knowledge will be applied and deepened through work on the assignment and discussions about your progress and that of others.

. . .

It is really important that you prepare yourself for every session.
For details what needs to be prepared, please see Table 1.

Effort

You will gain 5 ECTS for this course. This equals approx. 125 hours workload, of which you need most to pass the course.

  • Joint sessions including follow-up: approx. 30 hours
  • Assignment (teamwork and individual contributions): approx. 90 hours

. . .

You need to work on the assignment between the sessions and prepare for the sessions (individually and as a team).

. . .

Please prepare your schedule accordingly.

Grading

The grade is based on the evaluation of the assignment (seminar paper).

  • The seminar paper is created individually
  • The paper must be handed in at the end of the semester
  • Interim and final presentations are mandatory for getting feedback on the progress and sharing your knowledge
  • Presentations will not be graded
