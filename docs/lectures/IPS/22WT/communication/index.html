Reading exercise

Let‚Äôs do some reading.

Group A

Psychological flexibility

Read Kashdan (2010) (you might stop at Recommendations for Future Research)

  • Understand the concepts psychological health, psychological flexibility, ego-resiliency and their effects
  • Summarize the critical factors that influence the likelihood of psychological flexibility
  • Discuss the concepts in the group, clarify any issues of understanding and discuss surprising findings
  • Find an example you can use to explain the concepts and related findings to your boss (who is not literate in psychology)
  • Prepare a brief presentation (concepts, findings, example; approx. 10 minutes)

Group B

The benefit of being present

Read Brown and Ryan (2003), you might skim over the description of the methods of the studies and focus on the findings

  • Understand the concepts mindfulness, self-awareness, self-regulation, psychological well-being and their interrelations
  • Discuss the factors and their effects in the group and clarify any issues of understanding
  • Find an example/examples you can use to explain the findings to your boss (who is not literate in psychology)
  • Prepare a brief presentation (approx. 10 minutes) explaining the concepts and answering following questions
    • When is mindfulness compromised?
    • How do mindfulness and well-being relate?
    • How can mindfulness be cultivated?
    • What was the most surprising finding for you?

Group C

Acceptance and Commitment Therapy

Read Hayes et al. (2006)

  • Understand the concept of acceptance and commitment therapy (ACT), its theoretical roots (i.e., relational frame theory, RFT) and how it relates to other forms of cognitive-behavior therapy
  • Discuss what psychological inflexibility is from a RFT point of view, how it emerges and how ACT processes may establish psychological flexibility
  • Find an example/examples you can use to explain the findings to your boss
  • Prepare a short presentation (approx. 10 minutes) on the following questions
    • What is RFT and ACT?
    • How is psychological flexibility established according to ACT?
    • What has research found out?
    • What was the most surprising finding for you?

Group D

Psychological flexibility and organizational behavior

Read Bond, Hayes, and Barnes-Holmes (2013)

  • Understand the concepts psychological flexibility, relational frame theory, organizational behavior, organizational development and their interrelations
  • Discuss the concepts in the group and clarify any issues of understanding
  • Find an example/examples you can use to explain the findings to your boss
  • Prepare a short presentation (approx. 10 minutes) on the following questions
    • Why is psychological flexibility so important at work?
    • How can psychological flexibility improve organizational development?
    • How does psychological flexibility relate to individual characteristics
    • What was the most surprising finding for you?

Challenges

You want to learn how to give (and receive) more helpful feedback? Here are three challenges that might help you along the way.

  • Level 1: Start a feedback diary. Note situations where you had to give someone praise or criticism. Write down what you said. Analyse if your feedback was radically candid. Try to reframe your non-radically candid praise or criticism so that it‚Äôs more sincere and specific.
  • Level 2: Give feedback, multiple times. Practice radical candor by applying the framework and the practices outlined in the workbook. Reflect on how the techniques have improved your conversations and realtionships.
  • Level 3: Spread the word. Teach your team at work, a group of friends or your family about the radical candor framework (e.g., using the one-pager and the workbook. Introduce Whoops-a-Daisy & the Killer Whale and practice it at least once a week over the upcoming weeks (remember the CORE-model. Reflect on how the feedback culture has changed.
